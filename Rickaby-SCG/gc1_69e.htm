Of God and His Creatures

"The understanding can understand many things together, taking them as one, but not many things together, taking them as many. By 'taking them as one or many' I mean, by one or by several intellectual presentations. . . . . Whatsoever things therefore the mind can understand by one presentation, it can understand together. Hence God sees all things by one thing, which is His essence." -- St Thomas, Sum.Theol. I, q. 85, art. 4.
